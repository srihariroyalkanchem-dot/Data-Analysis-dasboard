# Data-Analysis-dasboard
Sales of bikes and cars in different ages (create interactive dashboard by microsoft excel)
#objective of the project
The main objective of this project is to analyze how various demographic, socioeconomic, and lifestyle factors—such as age, income, education, occupation, and commute distance—influence a customer’s decision to purchase a bike.
#KPI information
Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) Description
Bike Purchase Rate
Percentage of customers who purchased a bike out of the total dataset. This helps measure overall conversion.
Average Income of Buyers vs Non-Buyers
Compares income levels to understand whether higher or lower income groups are more likely to purchase bikes.
Age Group Purchase Distribution
Shows which age categories (Young, Middle Age, Old) are more inclined to buy bikes.
Commute Distance Impact
Analyzes how commute distance influences bike purchasing behavior.
Occupation-wise Purchase Rate
Identifies which professions have higher bike purchase rates.
Region-wise Sales Distribution
Helps understand geographic trends in bike purchases.
Home Ownership vs Purchase Behavior
Examines whether owning a home affects the likelihood of buying a bike.

# Project insight
Middle-aged customers show a higher tendency to purchase bikes compared to younger and older groups, indicating this segment as a key target audience.
Customers with moderate to high income levels are more likely to buy bikes, suggesting affordability plays an important role in the decision.
Individuals with short to moderate commute distances (e.g., 0–5 miles) are more inclined to purchase bikes, likely for convenience and cost-effectiveness.
Professionals and skilled workers demonstrate higher purchase rates compared to clerical or manual workers.
Cars Owned vs Bike Purchase
Studies the relationship between number of cars owned and bike purchase decisions.
# conclusion
This project reveals that bike purchasing behavior is strongly influenced by a combination of demographic, economic, and lifestyle factors. Customers who are middle-aged, financially stable, and have shorter commute distances are more likely to purchase bikes. Additionally, factors such as occupation, region, and existing vehicle ownership further shape buying decisions.
